# MEDDPICC Scoring Reference

Use this guide when scoring each call element from 0–2.

## Metrics (M)
Does the prospect understand and articulate the quantified business impact of solving their problem?

- **0**: No numbers discussed. Pain is described qualitatively only ("it's slow", "it's painful").
- **1**: Some numbers mentioned but not tied to business impact (e.g., "we have 50 reps" without linking to revenue impact).
- **2**: Clear quantified impact established (e.g., "we're losing ~$200K/quarter in rep productivity", "our close rate is 18% and we need to get to 25%").

## Economic Buyer (E)
Has the person who controls or approves the budget been identified and/or engaged?

- **0**: No mention of who owns the budget or signs the deal.
- **1**: Economic buyer named but not engaged ("my VP of Sales would need to approve this").
- **2**: Economic buyer is on the call, or champion has confirmed their position and interest.

## Decision Criteria (D)
Are the criteria by which they will evaluate and choose a solution clearly understood?

- **0**: No discussion of how they'll make the decision.
- **1**: General criteria mentioned ("we care about ease of use, integrations") but not prioritized or specific.
- **2**: Specific, ranked criteria are clear — and ideally, you understand where you're strong vs. weak.

## Decision Process (D)
Is the buying/approval workflow understood — who's involved, in what order, over what timeline?

- **0**: No discussion of process.
- **1**: Vague ("we'll need to get a few people involved") without specifics.
- **2**: Clear multi-step process mapped (e.g., "pilot with 5 reps → demo to VP → legal → sign before Q3 close").

## Paper Process (P)
Are the procurement, legal, security, or IT requirements known?

- **0**: Not discussed at all.
- **1**: Acknowledged that there will be a process ("we do have a procurement team") without details.
- **2**: Specific requirements surfaced (MSA needed, security review by InfoSec, net-30 terms required, etc.).

## Identify Pain (I)
Is the core business pain — the real reason they're looking — clearly surfaced and agreed upon?

- **0**: Surface-level or feature-driven conversation. No real pain identified.
- **1**: Pain mentioned but not explored deeply or confirmed as the primary driver.
- **2**: Root cause of pain clearly articulated by the prospect, with clear stakes if it goes unresolved.

## Champion (C)
Is there an internal advocate who is actively selling on your behalf inside the account?

- **0**: No champion identified.
- **1**: Someone seems friendly and engaged but hasn't taken any internal action on your behalf.
- **2**: Champion has shared internal information, arranged access to stakeholders, or actively promoted you internally.

## Competition (C)
Are you aware of who else the prospect is evaluating, and how you compare in their minds?

- **0**: Competition not discussed.
- **1**: Competition acknowledged ("we're talking to a couple of vendors") but not named or detailed.
- **2**: Specific competitors named, evaluation status known, and you understand where you stand relative to them.

---

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 14–16 | Highly qualified. Strong pipeline candidate. |
| 10–13 | Solid deal with gaps. Know what to work on next call. |
| 6–9   | Early stage or stalling. Needs deliberate MEDDPICC work. |
| 0–5   | Poorly qualified. Determine if it's worth progressing. |
